For instance, if you detected that there were two potential options that you couldn't really tell apart, you could just flip a coin to pick and be done with it. This would make more sense since the tradeoff between time spent and marginal utility gain becomes bad very quickly.

To get a sense of why this might be the case, consider bees. Bees need to make decisions about where to migrate to a different colony, and to do that, they need to somehow convince all the bees to move in the same direction at the same time.
