This is odd- most of the time we would assume that you would be allowed to have a state vector that is an arbitrary superposition- that is, an arbitrary additive combination- of basis vectors. Another seemingly arbitrary restriction is that the magnitude of the vector in any given direction can only be 0 or 1. Why can't we choose from the integers, or the real numbers, or complex numbers, or quaternions, or some other crazy mathematical object?

I bring this up not because these restrictions are a defect of the model (they aren't), but because they reveal to us an opportunity to explore generalizations of our current model. A new model built this way could be effective in explaining a broader range of physical phenomena without having to overhaul too many of the techniques we've already developed.
